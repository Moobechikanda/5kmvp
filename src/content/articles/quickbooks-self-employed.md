# QuickBooks Self-Employed Review: Accounting Made Simple

Tax time as a solopreneur used to mean weeks of stress, shoeboxes of receipts, and overpriced accountants. QuickBooks Self-Employed changes the game with automated tracking designed specifically for freelancers and independent contractors.

## The Core Problem

As a solopreneur, you face unique financial challenges:

- **Personal/business blurring**: Expenses mix
- **Self-employment tax**: 15.3% extra
- **Quarterly estimates**: Required payments
- **Deductions missed**: Money left on table
- **Audit risk**: Poor records

QuickBooks Self-Employed solves all of these.

## Key Features

### 1. Automatic Expense Tracking

Connect your:
- Bank accounts
- Credit cards
- PayPal

Every transaction imports automatically. Swipe to categorize:

- Business
- Personal
- Split (partial business)

**Value**: Saves 5-10 hours monthly

### 2. Mileage Tracking

**The killer feature.**

When enabled, QuickBooks tracks every drive via GPS. Swipe to categorize:
- Business trip
- Personal trip

**Value**: Average deduction $500-2,000 annually

### 3. Tax Estimation

Real-time tax liability:
- Calculates self-employment tax
- Estimates quarterly payments
- Shows refund/owe status

Never be surprised at tax time.

### 4. Schedule C Preparation

Export:
- Income summary
- Expense categories
- Ready for filing

**Value**: Saves hours at tax prep

### 5. Invoicing

Basic but functional:
- Create and send invoices
- Track payments
- Connect to payment processor

### 6. Receipt Capture

Snap photos of receipts:
- Mobile app
- Auto-matches to transactions
- Stores for audit proof

## Limitations to Understand

### Not Full Accounting Software

Missing:
- Double-entry bookkeeping
- Inventory management
- Payroll
- Advanced reporting

For growing businesses, upgrade to QuickBooks Online.

### Bank Sync Delays

Bank connections can lag 1-2 days for some institutions.

### Mileage Battery Use

GPS tracking uses battery—keep charger handy.

### Limited to US

QuickBooks Self-Employed is US-only. International solopreneurs need alternatives.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Not Connecting All Accounts**: Missing transactions = missed deductions
2. **Ignoring Mileage**: The biggest deduction for many
3. **Late Categorization**: Stay current daily
4. **Mixing Personal**: Keep separate when possible
5. **Not Backing Up**: Download reports quarterly

## Tips for Maximum Savings

### Mileage Strategies

- Track every business drive
- Log client locations
- Record purpose of trip
- Combine trips when possible

### Expense Categories

- Home office: Square footage × $5/sq ft
- Internet: Business percentage
- Phone: Business percentage
- Software: Fully deductible

### Quarterly Estimates

Pay on time to avoid penalties:
- April 15
- June 15
- September 15
- January 15
